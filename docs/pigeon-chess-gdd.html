Vision & Design Pillars

Chess is the most elegant game ever devised. Pigeon Chess is what happens when you let pigeons play it.

Pigeon Chess is a web-based and Steam roguelike chess game where every match is shaped by a chaotic, community-driven modifier system. No two games are ever the same. Players bring skill, the pigeons bring entropy.

The game respects the strategic foundations of chess while systematically dismantling them — one absurd modifier at a time. It targets chess players who are bored, non-chess players who are curious, and anyone who has ever wanted to watch a bishop gain sentience and walk off the board.

Design Pillars

🎲 Pillar 1 — Controlled Chaos Every modifier must be legible at a glance, no matter how absurd. Players should understand what's happening to them and laugh, not be confused. Chaos is the aesthetic. Clarity is the discipline.
📈 Pillar 2 — Skill Always Matters Even in maximum chaos mode, a better chess player should win more often than not. Modifiers shift the odds; they don't eliminate agency. The roguelike is a strategic layer, not a slot machine.
🐦 Pillar 3 — The Flock is Alive The game world feels inhabited. Pigeons are not a UI skin — they are characters. They have names. They have opinions. They land on squares when you don't want them to. The board is their city.
🌍 Pillar 4 — Community is the Content Puzzles, cosmetics, modifier suggestions, and seasonal events are shaped by the player community. The game grows because players grow it. Systems are built to enable this from day one.

Story & Narrative

📖 Premise You are Viktor Crumb, once the world's most feared chess grandmaster. Undefeated for eleven years. Then, at the World Championship, a flock of pigeons descended on the board mid-match — and you made the wrong move. You lost. You were disgraced. The chess world cast you out.

Years later, you find a strange board in a flea market. It pulses. It coos. When you place a piece on it, the pigeons come. And they change everything.

Narrative Structure

The Story Mode is structured as a roguelike campaign: Viktor must fight through the five "Grandmaster Towers" — each a gauntlet of increasingly unhinged opponents — to reclaim his title at the World Invitational. Each run through a Tower is a roguelike run: you pick modifiers, battle opponents, unlock lore, and accumulate the cursed power of the board.

Tone of Writing

Dialogue is dry, deadpan, and self-aware. Viktor takes chess desperately seriously. Everyone around him treats the chaos as completely normal. The humor is in the gap.

📝 Sample Dialogue Viktor: "There is a pigeon sitting on my queen."
The Prodigy: "I know. She's been there since last Tuesday. Her name is Margaret. She's ranked 1,400 ELO."
Viktor: "That is not possible."
The Prodigy: "Margaret, it's your turn."
[The pigeon moves a rook.]

Core Game Loop

The Match Loop

Every Match — In Order

  1. Lobby / Draft Phase — Players enter the modifier draft. Based on mode, they pick, ban, or budget modifiers from their unlocked pool.
  2. Board Setup — The board is generated based on active modifiers. Tile mutations, fog overlays, asymmetric armies are applied before piece placement.
  3. Match — Standard chess with active modifiers. Cooldown abilities are available via UI. Mid-match events (pigeon landings, tile eruptions) trigger on defined intervals or conditions.
  4. Resolution — Win/loss/draw determined. ELO updated (ranked). XP and currency awarded.
  5. Post-Match — Replay available. Highlight moments auto-clipped. In Story/Roguelike: next node on the run map is revealed.

The Roguelike Run Loop (Story & Roguelike Mode)

One Run — Start to End

  1. Run Start — Player begins with a base modifier loadout (1 free pick, 0 curses). Starting deck of available modifiers drawn from unlocked pool.
  2. Run Map — A branching path of nodes: matches, events, shops, and rest sites (modifier swaps). Player chooses their route.
  3. Between Matches — Earn Feathers (run currency). Visit shops to buy/sell modifiers. Remove curses for a price. Find relics.
  4. Boss Match — End of each Tower. Boss has unique modifiers pre-applied that the player cannot ban. High stakes, high reward.
  5. Run End (Win or Loss) — Unlock permanent meta-progression rewards. Expand the modifier pool. Unlock new relics, Viktor upgrades, and cosmetics.

Player Engagement Loop

Session (20–40 min)
Daily (XP + Quest)
Weekly (Battle Pass)
Seasonal (Ranked)

Modifier System

Modifiers are the heart of Pigeon Chess. They are categorized by type, tier, and cost. Each modifier has a point cost in the Auction system, a rarity tier, and a Curse rating (0–3) that contributes to Viktor's "Madness Meter" during roguelike runs.

Modifier Categories

Category A — Board Mutations (Tile-Based)

The Spreading Mold
🟫 Board Mutation · Tier S
Every 5 turns, one random tile becomes Moldy. Pieces on Moldy tiles lose 1 movement square per turn until the tile is "aired out" by a bishop or queen passing through.
Cost: 4pts · Curse: 1 · Mid-match trigger
Lava Squares
🟥 Board Mutation · Tier A
Four random squares become Lava at match start. Any piece landing on Lava is immediately captured. Lava moves clockwise one square every 10 turns.
Cost: 3pts · Curse: 1 · Persistent
Trapdoor Tiles
⬛ Board Mutation · Tier B
Three tiles are secretly designated trapdoors at match start. The first piece to land on each trapdoor falls through and reappears on the opposite side of the board.
Cost: 2pts · Curse: 0 · Hidden
Frozen Tundra
🟦 Board Mutation · Tier B
The top two rows of each side are frozen. Pieces entering the frozen zone move at half speed (every other turn). Pawns that reach the frozen zone are stuck for 2 turns before promoting.
Cost: 2pts · Curse: 0 · Persistent

Category B — Cursed Modifiers

The Madness
☠️ Curse · Tier S
Every 7 turns, one of YOUR pieces makes a random legal move on its own. You do not choose. The piece does. It seems happy about it. +2 Feathers per match.
Cost: -3pts (grants points) · Curse: 3
Pigeon Infestation
🐦 Curse · Tier A
A pigeon named Gerald lands on one of your pieces each turn. Gerald blocks that piece from moving until YOU click him off (costs your action). Gerald has favorites. He always comes back.
Cost: -2pts (grants points) · Curse: 2
Opposite Day
🔄 Curse · Tier B
Your pieces move in the opposite direction of what you click for the first 5 turns of the match. No exceptions. The UI will warn you. The pieces will not care.
Cost: -1pt (grants points) · Curse: 1
The Heavy Crown
👑 Curse · Tier A
Your King moves one square slower than normal (must spend 2 turns to move 1 square). He is very important and moves accordingly. Checkmate conditions unchanged.
Cost: -2pts (grants points) · Curse: 2

Category C — Piece Ability Cooldowns (Active Powers)

Royal Decree
⚡ Active Ability · King · Cooldown: 8 turns
Activate: Your King declares a "No Fly Zone" on any row or column. No opponent pieces may enter that line for 3 turns. Pigeons are exempt. The King cannot enforce pigeon compliance.
Cost: 4pts · Curse: 0
Bishop's Confession
⚡ Active Ability · Bishop · Cooldown: 5 turns
Activate: Choose one opponent piece. That piece must reveal its intended next move to you before making it (for 2 turns). Opponent sees the confession icon. They know you know.
Cost: 3pts · Curse: 0
Knight's Gambit
⚡ Active Ability · Knight · Cooldown: 6 turns
Activate: Your Knight may make ONE non-standard move this turn — it can move like a rook for this turn only. The Knight is aware this is illegal and seems thrilled about it.
Cost: 3pts · Curse: 0
Pawn Strike
⚡ Active Ability · Pawn · Cooldown: 4 turns
Activate on any pawn: That pawn can capture backwards this turn only. No promotion is awarded for this capture. The pawn will look very surprised at itself afterward.
Cost: 2pts · Curse: 0

Category D — Fog of War Variants

Dark Board
🌑 Fog of War · Tier S
You can only see squares within 2 squares of any of your own pieces. The rest of the board is hidden. Your opponent is in the dark too. Pigeons are always visible. They don't respect fog.
Cost: 4pts · Curse: 1 · Symmetric
Ghost Pieces
👻 Fog of War · Tier A
Both players see phantom decoy pieces on the board — 2 fake pieces per side that appear real until moved through. Only you know which of your pieces are real. Hover doesn't help.
Cost: 3pts · Curse: 0

Category E — Asymmetric Armies

The Flock Army
🐦 Asymmetric · Tier S
One player replaces all knights with "Pigeons" — pieces that move like knights but also randomly hop to any empty square every 5 turns. Both players can see where the pigeon will land next. The pigeon announces it 2 turns in advance.
Cost: 5pts · Curse: 0
The Peasant Revolt
♟️ Asymmetric · Tier A
One player starts with 12 pawns instead of the standard 8. No knights, no bishops. Just an overwhelming tide of extremely determined pawns. The other player keeps standard pieces.
Cost: 3pts · Curse: 0

⚖️ Balance Philosophy Every modifier must pass the "Laugh Test" (is the chaos fun?) and the "Rematch Test" (do players want to play again rather than quit?). Modifiers that consistently produce unfun states — where one player is helpless rather than challenged — must be nerfed or redesigned. Balance patches ship on a 2-week cadence.

Roguelike Meta-Progression

Run Structure

Each run takes place within one Tower (Story Mode) or an Endless Gauntlet (Roguelike Mode). Runs are structured as a branching node map — inspired by Slay the Spire — with the following node types:

Node TypeIconWhat HappensFrequency
Match♟️Standard match with current modifier loadout vs. AI or player60%
The Flock Event🐦Narrative event with 2–3 choices. Gain modifier, lose modifier, face a cursed bargain15%
The Roost (Shop)🏪Spend Feathers to add/remove modifiers, buy relics, unlock Viktor upgrades15%
The Perch (Rest)🌿Remove one curse OR preview next opponent's modifier loadout10%
Boss Match👑End-of-Tower boss. Pre-set cursed modifiers + unique boss mechanic1 per Tower

Currencies & Resources

🪶 Feathers (Run Currency)

Earned per match. Spent at The Roost. Lost on run end. Used to buy modifiers, remove curses, and purchase relics mid-run.

📿 Relics

Passive items that persist for the entire run. Max 3 active at a time. Examples: The Bent Pawn (pawns promote one rank early), Margaret's Feather (one pigeon interference negated per match), The Crumpled Draw Offer (draw once per run without penalty).

🏆 Prestige Tokens (Meta Currency)

Earned by completing runs regardless of win/loss. Spent in the permanent meta-progression tree to unlock new modifiers, new relics, Viktor's storyline upgrades, and cosmetics.

😤 The Madness Meter

Tracks total Curse rating of active modifiers. As Madness fills, Viktor's dialogue becomes increasingly unhinged, new hidden modifiers unlock, and the board begins exhibiting unexplained visual anomalies. Full Madness triggers the "Full Flock" secret modifier.

Permanent Meta-Progression Tree

Viktor's Redemption Tree is a persistent skill web unlocked with Prestige Tokens. It is split into three branches:

🌳 The Redemption Tree — Branches The Tactician Branch: Unlocks deeper modifier synergies, increases point budget in draft, unlocks advanced chess rule variant modifiers.

The Survivor Branch: Unlocks more rest nodes, better relic drops, curse removal discounts, pigeon deterrent abilities.

The Flock Whisperer Branch: Embraces chaos — unlocks higher Curse modifiers, grants Feather bonuses for cursed play, unlocks pigeon companion cosmetics and the secret Flock Army modifiers.

Ranking & Competitive System

ELO System — The Pecking Order

Pigeon Chess uses a modified Glicko-2 ELO system (the same model used by chess.com and lichess) with a modifier-awareness adjustment. Because modifiers affect match outcomes, a small Chaos Coefficient is applied to reduce ELO volatility in high-modifier matches.

🦉The Owlmaster1700–1999Exclusive title + pigeon companion

Ranked Seasons

  • Each season lasts 3 months with a themed modifier pool (e.g., Season 1: Winter Flock — ice tile modifiers added to ranked pool).
  • Season reset: ELO soft-resets by 30% toward the median at season end.
  • Top 100 players on a live leaderboard visible in-game.
  • Ranked matches require a minimum 10 calibration games before ELO assignment.

Community Systems & Puzzles

Puzzle System

Community puzzles are positions + modifier loadouts that present a defined challenge. They may ask: "Win in 3 moves with The Spreading Mold active" or "Survive 5 turns against The Flock Army."

Puzzle Creator Tool

  • Drag-and-drop board editor
  • Set any modifier combination
  • Define win condition (checkmate, capture, survive N turns)
  • Set difficulty tag (Egg / Hatchling / Grandmaster)
  • Submit for community review (voting system)

Puzzle Discovery

  • Daily Featured Puzzle (editorially selected)
  • Hot / New / Top tabs
  • Filter by modifier type, difficulty, piece count
  • Puzzle streaks for daily engagement
  • Creator attribution + follower system

Community Cosmetics Pipeline

Players can submit cosmetic designs (board themes, piece skins, modifier card art) through a Community Workshop. Approved designs enter the store with revenue sharing (suggested: 30% to creator). This reduces art production burden for a solo/tiny team dramatically.

🎨 Cosmetic Categories Board Themes — Full board visual reskin (tiles, borders, frame)
Piece Sets — Complete or partial piece reskins (e.g., all pigeons, medieval, space)
Modifier Card Art — Illustrated card faces for the modifier draft UI
Pigeon Companions — Named pigeon sidekicks with idle animations on your UI
Viktor Outfits — Cosmetic changes to Viktor's portrait in Story Mode
Captured Piece Effects — Particle/animation effects when pieces are taken

Monetization

⚖️ Iron Rule No modifier, relic, or gameplay advantage may ever be purchased with real money. The modifier unlock system is meta-progression earned through play only. Every purchasable item is cosmetic. Violating this rule destroys the competitive community.

Cosmetics StoreIndividual board themes, piece sets, companions. No randomized loot boxes.$2.99–$14.99Rotating weekly

What is Never Sold

  • Modifiers of any kind
  • ELO boosts or ranked advantages
  • Loot boxes or gacha mechanics
  • Pay-to-skip story progression
  • Exclusive game modes

Battle Pass Structure (Per Season)

50 tiers. Free track contains: XP boosts, Feather bonuses (run currency), 1 board theme, 1 modifier card skin. Premium track adds: full piece set, 2 pigeon companions, Viktor outfit, animated board effects, exclusive title, season trophy.

Technical Architecture

🏗️ Architecture Principle Design for web-first, optimize for Steam via Electron or Tauri wrapper. A single codebase serves both platforms. Mobile is not in scope at launch but the architecture must not preclude it.

Recommended Stack

LayerTechnologyRationale
FrontendReact + TypeScriptComponent-based UI, large ecosystem, easy Steam wrapper integration
Game EngineCustom chess engine (TypeScript) or chess.jsChess logic must be server-authoritative; chess.js is battle-tested
RenderingPixiJS or Phaser2D canvas rendering for board animations, pigeon landings, tile mutations
BackendNode.js + Express / FastifyReal-time matchmaking, game state, ELO calculations
Real-timeSocket.IO or native WebSocketsMove synchronization, chat, pigeon event broadcasts
DatabasePostgreSQL + RedisPostgres for persistent data; Redis for session state and matchmaking queues
AuthAuth0 or ClerkOAuth (Google, Steam, Discord), JWT session management
SteamTauri (Rust) wrapperLightweight, fast, wraps web app for Steam distribution
HostingRailway / Render (MVP) → AWS/GCP (scale)Low-cost for solo dev, scalable for growth

Critical Systems to Architect First

  1. Server-Authoritative Chess Engine — All move validation must happen server-side. No client-side trust. Anti-cheat starts here.
  2. Modifier State Machine — Each modifier is a discrete state object with hooks into the game loop (on_turn_start, on_piece_moved, on_capture, on_match_end). This architecture lets modifiers be added without rewriting core logic.
  3. Matchmaking Service — ELO-based pairing with modifier-awareness (pair players with similar unlocked pools in casual modes).
  4. Replay System — Every match recorded as a deterministic event stream. Essential for puzzles, anti-cheat review, and highlight sharing.

Roadmap & Scope

🎯 Solo/Tiny Team Reality Check This is an ambitious game. The roadmap below is designed so that each phase is a shippable, playable product. Do not attempt to build everything before launching. A functional Phase 1 with 20 modifiers and 2 modes will teach you more than 2 years of solo development.
Phase 0 — Months 1–2
Prototype & Proof of Concept

Build a playable chess game in the browser. No modifiers. No accounts. Just chess that works reliably. Validate the chess engine, the board renderer, and real-time multiplayer sync. This is the foundation everything else sits on. Do not skip it.
